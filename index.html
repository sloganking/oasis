Oasis

A heart-centered, sex-positive community house in Arizona

Welcome Aboard

Oasis is a five-bedroom community house where open-hearted people live, grow, and explore together. We're building a home that honors both depth and play — the kind of place where a quiet evening might turn into a spontaneous couch pile.

We're in Coronado, Phoenix AZ — 10 minutes from downtown, 9 minutes from Sky Harbor airport, and central to everything happening in the valley. Nothing is more than a 30-minute drive.

The house carries an ocean and boat theme — Captain Lynn, our homeowner, loves the sea and all things nautical. But each room is yours to make your own. Bring your vibe, your art, your altar — whatever makes a space feel like home.

We have a nice outdoor space — perfect for morning stretches, evening hangs, or whatever your housemates feel like getting into together.

Our Values

We're a small crew, not a commune with a rulebook. But these are the currents that run through the house:

Close & Connected

We want this to feel like family. Warm, juicy, genuinely interested in each other's lives. The kind of house where you find out about cool things because your housemates can't stop talking about them.

Consent-Aware & Boundary-Respecting

Clear communication, respected boundaries, enthusiastic consent. This is the container that lets everything else be free.

Erotically Alive & Clothing-Optional

Sex-positivity isn't just tolerated here — it's woven in. We're comfortable with bodies, with desire, with openness. Nudity is normal. We host gatherings, and housemates need to be on board with that.

Growth-Oriented

We want housemates who have done personal growth work or are actively in it — ISTA, tantra, embodiment retreats, Landmark, Tony Robbins, festivals, whatever your path. People already on a journey.

Up to Something (Bonus)

Entrepreneurs, creators, practitioners, people on a mission — we love that energy. Not a requirement, but if you're building something, you'll feel at home here.

Spontaneous & Inviting

Someone stretches in the yard and texts "come join." Someone finds a workshop and says "who's in?" Life here is better shared.
